Why a Collision Specialist

Collision repair is structural — not just cosmetic.

The thing most San Diego drivers don't know: a car that looks "fixed" after a collision can still have hidden frame damage that affects safety, resale value, and how your car handles in the next accident.

Certified collision shops measure the frame, replace structural parts to factory specs, and recalibrate safety systems. That's the difference between a real repair and a cosmetic patch.

Common Questions

Collision Repair FAQ.

How do I know if my collision damage is serious?
Visible body damage is one thing. Frame damage, suspension misalignment, and bent inner panels often hide behind a "fixable" exterior. A teardown estimate at a certified collision shop is the only reliable way to know what is actually under the panels.
Does insurance always cover collision repair?
If you carry collision coverage, yes — minus your deductible. If you are not at fault, the at-fault driver's liability insurance should cover it. We help San Diego drivers navigate which policy applies and how the claim process starts.
Can I drive my car after a collision?
If the vehicle drives straight, brakes normally, and has all lights working, usually yes. If the steering pulls, brakes feel different, anything is dragging, or warning lights are on, get it towed. Send photos and we will tell you if it is safe.
What is ADAS calibration and why does it matter after a collision?
Modern cars have driver assistance systems — cameras, radar, lane keep, automatic braking. After collision repair (or even a windshield replacement), those systems need to be recalibrated. Skipping this is a safety risk and can fail inspection.
How long does collision repair take in San Diego?
Light collision (single panel) can be one to two weeks. Major collision with structural work, parts on order, and ADAS calibration can run four to six weeks or longer. We give realistic timelines based on the damage scope, not optimistic ones.
Do I have to use the insurance company's recommended shop?
In California, no. You choose where your vehicle is repaired. Direct Repair Program (DRP) shops can move faster on insurer paperwork, but they have a volume contract with the insurer. An independent shop works for you.
