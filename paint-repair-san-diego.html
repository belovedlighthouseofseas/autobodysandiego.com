Why a Paint Specialist

Paint is its own craft — not an afterthought to body work.

A lot of San Diego "auto body" shops will quote you on paint as if it's a side service. Paint specialists do nothing else. That means controlled spray environments, proper primer prep, and color matching that's been done thousands of times on the same paint codes.

The difference shows up in two places: how the panel looks under sunlight, and how the paint holds up two years from now. Specialist work blends seamlessly and lasts.

If the panel itself is damaged, we'll route you to collision repair first. Body work first, paint last — never the other way around.

Common Questions

Paint Repair FAQ.

How much does paint repair cost in San Diego?
It depends entirely on whether the damage is a single panel, multi-panel, or full repaint. Daylight photos help us tell you up front whether you are looking at a small touch-up or a larger refinish.
Can clear coat peeling be repaired without a full repaint?
Often yes — if the peeling is contained to one or two panels, those panels can be sanded, primed, repainted, and blended into the surrounding panels. Full-vehicle clear coat failure usually needs a full repaint.
How do you match the color exactly?
The paint specialist uses your vehicle's factory paint code combined with computerized color matching. Modern color tools account for the way paint shifts in San Diego sun. The result blends seamlessly into adjoining panels.
How long does a paint repair take?
Single-panel paint repairs typically finish in three to five business days. Multi-panel refinishes run a week or more. Full vehicle repaints can take two to three weeks including proper cure time.
Will the repaired paint match the rest of the car?
A specialist paint shop blends the new paint into adjacent panels rather than stopping at the panel edge. Done properly, the repair is visually invisible. Done poorly, it shows up under sunlight.
Does San Diego sun damage paint differently?
Yes. Coastal sun, salt air, and parking outdoors accelerate clear coat failure, oxidation, and fading — especially on roofs, hoods, and trunk lids. Hot spots near the coast see more paint work than inland areas.
