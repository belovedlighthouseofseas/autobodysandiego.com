# SESSION 2 — AutoBody San Diego Logo System + Visual Direction

## Turning the Logo Into a Real Brand Asset System

> Status: Locked, v1.0. Canonical reference for all logo work, color usage, typography, icon system, and design motifs. Read this before generating any logo variant or designing a UI component.

Session 1 locked the identity:

**AutoBody San Diego = the San Diego auto body traffic headquarters.**

Now Session 2 locks the visual system so future build work does not produce a random template website. The logo, colors, typography, icon marks, and design rules need to work across:

Website header · Homepage hero · Mobile navigation · Quote form · Social media · Google Business Profile · Favicon · Printed flyers · Business cards · Vehicle decals · Future service-area pages · Future brand network pages

The current logo is strong as a **hero logo**, but Session 2 turns it into a usable **brand system**.

---

## 1. The Current Logo Assessment

The current generated logo has strong pieces:

**Strong points:**
- Matches the black / chrome / blue family DNA
- Feels automotive
- Premium digital performance look
- Looks connected to BumperFix, PaintFix, CollisionFix, and CrashFix
- "AUTOBODY" text has authority
- Blue slash gives it movement
- "SAN DIEGO" line creates local identity
- Tagline clearly explains the service categories

**One important issue:** the current logo is too detailed to be the *only* logo.

Works well as: homepage hero, social banner, splash image, large website brand section, presentation/flyer header.

Too detailed for: mobile header, tiny favicon, small corner logo, footer logo, quote form header, Google Business Profile picture, business card corner.

So the correct move is not to throw it away. The correct move is to build a **logo family** around it.

## 2. Final Logo System Structure — six versions

### Version 1 — Full Hero Logo ✅ Done

Large cinematic logo. Used for: homepage hero, landing page hero, social cover image, website splash, brand presentation, large banner, vehicle graphic mockups.

Format:
**AUTOBODY**
**SAN DIEGO**
**Collision • Paint • Bumper • Insurance Help**

Visual details: car silhouette, chrome lettering, electric blue glow, blue repair slash, small SD shield, long horizontal accent lines.

### Version 2 — Website Header Logo ⏳ Needed

Simplified daily-use logo. For: website top navigation, mobile header, sticky header, footer, quote form top bar.

**Best choice:**
- Mobile header: **AUTOBODY SD**
- Desktop header: **AUTOBODY SAN DIEGO**

No tagline. No car silhouette. No tiny details. No complex glow. Must be readable at small size.

### Version 3 — Icon Mark ⏳ Needed

Compact symbol. For: favicon, app-style icon, social profile picture, watermark, button icon, quote form badge, map pin, review response profile image.

**Recommended:** **SD shield + blue slash**

Reason: "SD" is short, local, and clean. The shield gives authority. The slash connects visually to the Fix-brand universe.

### Version 4 — Flat Logo ⏳ Needed

Clean, non-3D version. For: documents, invoices, email signatures, business forms, printed paperwork, legal documents, simple website sections, black-and-white use.

Format: white-on-black, black-on-white, blue accent optional. No chrome, no glow, no 3D bevel, no complex reflections.

### Version 5 — Transparent PNG Logo ⏳ Needed

For: website overlays, hero sections, ads, flyers, video thumbnails, social posts, before-and-after image overlays.

Need: transparent full logo, transparent header logo, transparent SD icon.

### Version 6 — Social Square Logo ⏳ Needed

For: Facebook page, Instagram, TikTok, Google Business Profile, YouTube, review platform icons.

Layout: black square background, centered SD shield, small "AutoBody San Diego" below or around it, blue slash/accent.

Do NOT use the full long horizontal logo as a profile picture — it will shrink and become unreadable.

## 4. Final Logo Direction

**AutoBody San Diego should feel like the official local repair gateway, not a single repair niche.**

That means:
- Not too bumper-focused
- Not too paint-focused
- Not too collision-focused
- Not too aggressive like CrashFix
- Not too colorful like PaintFix

It should be: Official · Clean · Regional · Strong · Helpful · Automotive · Search-authority focused.

The logo should feel like the command center sitting above the service brands.

---

## 5. Color System (locked)

### Primary Color — Black

For: website background, logo background, hero sections, footer, cards, navigation.

Values: `#050505` · `#080A0D` · `#0B0F14`. Use mostly black and near-black, not pure gray.

### Primary Text — White / Metallic White

For: headlines, logo text, button text, main page copy.

Values: `#FFFFFF` · `#F2F4F7` · `#DDE3EA`

### Primary Accent — Electric Blue

For: buttons, lines, slashes, icons, glow accents, active states, form focus borders.

Values: `#009DFF` · `#007BFF` · `#00AEEF`

### Secondary Accent — Deep Blue

For: gradients, hover effects, shadow glows, secondary details.

Values: `#004CFF` · `#0057D9`

### Limited Accent — Red (used carefully)

Values: `#FF1E1E` · `#D60000`

For: accident help, emergency-style callouts, small slash detail, crash-related pages, warning notes.

AutoBody San Diego should not become red-dominant. Red belongs more to CrashFix.

---

## 6. Gradient Rules

### Good gradients

Black → deep blue · Chrome white → light gray · Electric blue glow → transparent · Dark navy → black.

### Bad gradients

Rainbow everywhere · Too much red · Cheap neon · Flat template blue · Over-glowing buttons · Random purple (unless tied to PaintFix).

The AutoBody San Diego visual world should be: black · chrome · blue · small red only when needed.

---

## 7. Typography System

### Display Font

For: hero headline, service page titles, large CTA sections, brand headings, damage selector titles.

Style: Wide · Bold · Automotive · Sharp · Compressed or extended · High-impact.

Direction examples: Anton-style · Bebas Neue-style · Orbitron-style · Eurostile-style · Agency-style · Rajdhani-style.

Best practical options:
- **Anton** for bold impact
- **Rajdhani** for futuristic readability
- **Orbitron** for tech/automotive accent — but use carefully

**Locked recommendation:**
- Use **Rajdhani** for the brand / interface feel
- Use **Inter** or **DM Sans** for body copy
- Alternative: use **Anton** for only major hero titles and **Inter** for everything else

### Body Font

For: paragraphs, forms, buttons, descriptions, FAQ, city pages, mobile copy.

Best choices: Inter · DM Sans · Sora · Montserrat.

**Locked recommendation:** Inter or DM Sans (currently DM Sans).

### Typography Rule

The logo can be futuristic. The website must be readable. Do not let the whole website become a hard-to-read racing poster.

## 8. Website Visual Direction

The site should not look like a basic repair shop. It should look like a **repair-routing dashboard**.

Visual language: Dark interface · Glassy cards · Blue glowing borders · Clean icons · Sharp diagonal section dividers · Large readable headers · Damage-type cards · Photo upload CTA · Local San Diego coverage map-style section · Service gateway tiles.

Think: automotive command center. Not directory. Not blog. Not cheap shop template.

---

## 9. Core UI Components

### Header

**Desktop:** Logo left · navigation center or right · CTA button right (**Get a Quote**)
**Mobile:** Logo left · menu icon right · sticky quote button or bottom CTA

Navigation items: Services · Repair Types · Insurance Help · Service Areas · Get a Quote

### Hero Section

Large logo or brand text · strong headline · short subheadline · primary CTA · secondary CTA · damage selector preview.

### Damage Selector Cards

Bumper Damage · Paint Damage · Collision Damage · Insurance Help · Not Sure.

Each card: icon, short title, one-sentence explanation, CTA link.

### Service Gateway Cards

Bumper Repair San Diego · Paint Repair San Diego · Collision Repair San Diego · Accident Repair Help.

Each card should visually feel connected but distinct.

### Quote CTA Block

Dark section with blue border/glow.
Headline: **Not sure what repair you need? Send photos first.**
CTA: **Upload Damage Photos**

### Local Coverage Grid

City/neighborhood links as clean pill buttons or cards.

### Footer

Logo · short description · service links · city links · contact CTA · legal/footer links.

---

## 10. Icon System

Icons should be simple, clean, automotive.

Needed icons: Bumper · Paint spray gun or droplet · Collision impact · Insurance document · Camera/photo upload · Map pin · Phone · Text message · Checkmark · Car silhouette · Estimate/document · Shield/authority.

Style: thin or medium line icons, white with blue accents, not cartoon, not overly detailed, consistent stroke width.

Avoid: random emoji icons, cheap clipart, overly colorful icons, generic stock icons that clash with the logo.

---

## 11. Imagery Direction

Potential images: close-up bumper scratch · paint peeling/clear coat issue · collision-damaged vehicle · technician inspecting damage · before-and-after repair · San Diego road/city background · car silhouette graphics · texture backgrounds (carbon fiber, dark metal, paint mist, blueprint grid).

Strongest direction is a **hybrid**: logo-style graphics + dark UI + real repair photos + before-and-after proof + service-specific damage examples.

**Image rule:** Use real repair/damage photos when proving services. Use futuristic graphics when building brand authority.

## 13. Mobile Design Rules

Most customers searching for auto body help will likely be on phones.

### Mobile header

Small logo · simple menu · visible quote CTA.

### Mobile homepage order

1. Headline
2. CTA buttons
3. Damage selector
4. Quote by photos
5. Service cards
6. Local coverage
7. Trust/education
8. Footer

### Mobile buttons

Large enough to tap · clear labels · no tiny text.

### Mobile forms

Short steps · large input fields · photo upload easy · phone/text CTA visible.

Do not put the giant full hero logo so large that it pushes the actual CTA too far down.

## 20. Visual Mistakes to Avoid

- Too much chrome everywhere
- Logo too large on mobile
- Tiny unreadable tagline in header
- Overusing red
- Looking like a racing game instead of repair authority
- Generic mechanic stock photos
- Cartoon icons
- Too dark without readable contrast
- A normal "About / Services / Contact" template with no routing system
- Every page visually identical without service-specific personality
- Using the full hero logo in every small space

The site must feel premium but usable.

---

## 21. Design Personality by Page Type

Each page can share the same system but slightly shift emphasis.

- **Homepage** — Command center feeling. Broad, official, regional.
- **Bumper Repair Page** — Blue, clean, cosmetic, fast, practical.
- **Paint Repair Page** — More color/finish energy, but still controlled.
- **Collision Repair Page** — More serious, inspection-focused, structural confidence.
- **Accident / Insurance Page** — Calm, guidance-focused, slightly more red/blue authority.
- **City Pages** — Local, straightforward, SEO-readable, not overdesigned.

---

## 22. Final Visual Direction Statement

**AutoBody San Diego uses a premium black, chrome-white, and electric-blue automotive visual system designed to feel like a San Diego auto body command center. The logo family includes a full cinematic hero logo, a simplified website header logo, an SD shield icon, and a flat document-ready mark. The website design should use sharp blue repair slashes, thin electric divider lines, glassy dark cards, strong mobile-first typography, and clear damage-routing components. The brand must feel connected to BumperFix USA, PaintFix USA, CollisionFix USA, and CrashFix USA while remaining broader, more official, and more locally authoritative.**
