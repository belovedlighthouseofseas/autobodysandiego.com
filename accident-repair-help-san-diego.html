First Steps After an Accident

A clear path forward when everything feels chaotic.

01

Document the scene

Photos of all vehicles, license plates, license, insurance card, and the surrounding intersection.

02

Get a police report

Even for minor accidents in San Diego. The report number protects you in any future dispute.

03

Don't admit fault

"I'm sorry," even when polite, can be used against you later. Stick to the facts.

04

Notify your insurer

Open the claim. You don't have to commit to a shop yet — that's a separate decision.

05

Choose YOUR shop

In California, you choose where your car gets repaired. The insurance recommendation is optional.

06

Document hidden damage

Always assume there's more damage than what's visible. Get a real teardown estimate.

What Not to Sign

Three things to be careful with after a San Diego accident.

1. Quick-settlement releases. The at-fault party's insurance may offer a fast cash payout if you sign away your right to future claims. Don't — hidden damage and medical issues can surface days later.

2. "Network shop" direction. Insurers often steer you to a Direct Repair Program (DRP) shop. Those shops work for the insurer's volume contract, not you. You're allowed to pick any licensed shop.

3. Total-loss valuations without comparison. If your car is declared a total loss, the first valuation is rarely the final number. You have the right to negotiate with comparable San Diego market data.

Choosing a Shop

How to pick a body shop after an accident.

A

Pick by certification, not convenience

I-CAR Gold, OEM certifications, and brand-specific training matter more than location.

B

Ask about teardown

A real shop tears the car down before estimating. Anything else is guesswork.

C

Get OEM parts in writing

Especially for structural parts. Aftermarket sheet metal often fits worse and rusts faster.

D

Confirm ADAS calibration

Any car with cameras or radar needs calibration after panel or windshield work. Non-negotiable.

Common Questions

Accident Repair Help FAQ.

What should I do right after a car accident in San Diego?
Document everything: photos of all vehicles, license plates, license, insurance card, and the surrounding intersection. Get a police report — even for minor accidents. Do not admit fault. Notify your insurer to open the claim. You do not have to commit to a shop yet.
Do I have to use the body shop my insurance recommends?
No. California law lets you choose where your vehicle is repaired. Insurance recommendations are optional. Direct Repair Program shops have a volume contract with the insurer — useful for speed, but the shop's loyalty is to the contract, not always to you.
Should I get a quick settlement check from the other driver's insurance?
Be careful. Quick settlements often require you to sign away your right to future claims. Hidden vehicle damage and medical issues can surface days or weeks later. Get a real teardown estimate before signing anything.
What if I think the insurance estimate is too low?
Initial estimates almost always miss damage. Shops write "supplements" once the car is torn down and inner damage is visible. Insurers usually pay supplements, but the back-and-forth can delay your car. A specialist accident-help service walks you through it.
How do I know if the damage is more than what's visible?
You usually do not until teardown. Bent inner panels, broken brackets, deformed reinforcement bars, and hidden structural damage are common. Insist on a teardown estimate, not a walk-around estimate.
Can I send photos before deciding what to do?
Yes — that is the fastest path. A few photos let us tell you whether this is a cosmetic repair, a collision repair, or an insurance-claim path before you commit to anything.
