California Driver Rights

What insurers don't always tell you up front.

01

You choose the shop

Under California law, you select where your vehicle is repaired. Not your insurer.

02

Estimate disputes are normal

The first estimate is rarely the final number. Supplements happen during teardown.

03

OEM parts matter for safety

You can request OEM parts, especially for structural and safety components.

04

Diminished value applies

If you're not at fault, you may have a diminished-value claim — even after a "complete" repair.

05

Rental coverage is yours

If you carry rental coverage, you're entitled to use it during the full repair window.

06

You can dispute total-loss values

Initial offers are based on data the insurer chose. You can counter with comparable San Diego sales.

Supplements 101

Why the first estimate is almost never the final price.

Adjusters write estimates based on what they can see from a walk-around. They miss hidden damage almost every time — bent inner panels, broken brackets, sensors that need replacement, hidden structural deformation.

Once the shop tears the car down, they write a "supplement" requesting more parts and labor. A good shop handles supplements as a normal part of the workflow. The insurer almost always pays them, but the back-and-forth can delay your car.

AutoBody San Diego helps you understand what supplements should be requested, how to interpret the line items, and when to push back.

DRP vs Independent

Should you use the insurance "preferred" shop?

+

DRP shop: faster start

Direct Repair Program shops can usually start repairs without a separate insurer inspection.

-

DRP shop: insurer-aligned

The shop's volume contract is with the insurer. That can affect parts choices and supplement decisions.

+

Independent: customer-aligned

An independent shop works for you. Easier to push for OEM parts and proper repair.

-

Independent: slower start

You may need a separate adjuster inspection before work begins, adding a few days.
